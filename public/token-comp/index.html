FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Get answers to common questions about token compensation accounting

Token compensation accounting involves tracking and recording the fair value of token grants issued to employees or contractors as compensation, following US GAAP standards similar to traditional stock-based compensation.

We use a comprehensive valuation approach considering factors like market price (if available), comparable transactions, vesting schedules, liquidity restrictions, and market conditions at the grant date.

Token compensation expense should be recognized ratably over the vesting period, similar to stock-based compensation. The fair value determined at the grant date is expensed as the tokens vest.

Under the grant-date fair value method (similar to ASC 718), post-grant price changes typically don't affect the expense amount. The fair value is fixed at the grant date and expensed over the vesting period.

When an employee leaves before full vesting, previously recognized expense for unvested tokens is typically reversed (forfeiture accounting), reducing compensation expense in the period of departure.
